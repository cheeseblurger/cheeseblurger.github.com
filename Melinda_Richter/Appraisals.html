Appraisals

 

Why do I need an appraisal?

There are many different reasons for appraisals, each with its own approach to value. An appraisal may help protect you from loss, document a gift to a charitable institution, aid in distribution of assets, advise in the sale of an object or simply satisfy your curiosity about an object’s value.


Insurance: Coverage of fine art as listed personal property or in blanket coverage can protect you from  damage, loss, and deterioration or your artwork. If you don’t have up to date valuations, insurance companies may only compensate you for the original cost. An appraisal provides statements of value for replacement with future projections in mind so you can take advantage of appreciation in value when you go to replace or repair items.


Donation and Sale: A sound tax plan may benefit from donations of artwork as “Donations in kind” to charitable organizations such as public galleries, hospitals, or other non-profits and fundraisers. The amount realized through donation can be more than what is obtained though sale at auction. Recipients of your donations may require an appraisal before issuing a tax receipt. I can also help arrange donation of items to appropriate charities.


Equitable Estate Division: Artwork can be gifted to children and heirs. Knowing the value makes the distribution fair and you can be ensured that your heirs know the value of the bequests.


Fair Market Value: With factors such as age, condition, reputation of the artist, and current market conditions in mind, a Fair Market Value can be assigned to your artwork. This is a good first step when deciding what to do with a piece of art. You may then consider selling or gifting your valuables.

 

What is CPPAG?

CPPAG is a Canadian made, national, personal property appraisal organization comprising a network of accredited appraisers spanning from coast to coast. CPPAG provides personal property appraisal training and accreditation to its members who come from a diversity of backgrounds including: auctioneers, bailiffs, insurance industry, antique and equipment dealers, accounting and financial institutions and lawyers.
 

How often should I update

my appraisal?

It is recommended clients update their appraisals every five years. At the very least, a ten year schedule should be maintained, but for high value items whose values may fluctuate, a more frequent update is advisable.

What do I get in an appraisal report?

An appraisal usually consists of a detailed catalogue of the appraised objects, including photographs, descriptions, dimensions, and condition of the objects. Also included is a definition of value, methods, and scope of the appraisal, and any supporting documentation or research that is required to determine value.
